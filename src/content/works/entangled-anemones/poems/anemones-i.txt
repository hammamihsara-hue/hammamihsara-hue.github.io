i propagate anemones in still water
i blow on the surface to try to give them
something they don’t know yet to miss —  salted
dewy lovers at the very bottom of the red sea start the first
steps towards each other. back leg braced for impact, front leg
meandering. the sand echoes the procession, wafting up & flooding
down across the depths they smile at each other a wedding beat oh bride
blushes hotly & the air above the surface undulates ululating watch the ringed
fish dart in & out of home not yet aware that when picked up & bagged & forlorn
their mothers keen, sending them with small attachments i sweep & pick out & place in
mason jars of dead water & the fish are alone & the anemones starving & i, like all things, weep
